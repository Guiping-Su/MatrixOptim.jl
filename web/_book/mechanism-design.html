6.1 Mechanism Design

6.1.1 Desired Properties

According to (Mitridati, Kazempour, and Pinson 2019), following four desired properties are defined:

Definition 6.1 Efficiency The social welfare of the agents participating in the mechanism, with respect to the revealed preferences, is maximized, and no agent desires to deviate unilaterally from its dis- patch. In other words, the outcomes of social welfare maximization align with profit-maximization of agents.
Definition 6.2 Incentive compatibility Every agent can maximize its utility by acting according to its true preferences.
Definition 6.3 Budget balance (allocation efficiency) The community manager has neither financial deficit nor financial excess.
Definition 6.4 Group rationality No group of agents can benefit by splitting from the community.

Mitridati, Lesia, Jalal Kazempour, and Pierre Pinson. 2019. “Design and Game-Theoretic Analysis of Community-Based Market Mechanisms in Heat and Electricity Systems.” Omega, 102177.
